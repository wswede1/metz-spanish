# ¡Conoce a Colombia! — 10-Minute Student Walkthrough
**Presentation Script + Slide Notes**
*Estimated time: 10 minutes | Suggested tool: Canva or Google Slides*

---

## SLIDE 1 — Title
**Slide text:**
> ¡Conoce a Colombia!
> Your Digital Spanish Learning Hub
> Spanish 1 / Spanish 2 — Colombia Unit

**Speaker notes (30 sec):**
"Welcome to our Colombia unit digital hub. Everything you need for this unit — readings, grammar practice, vocabulary, cultural exploration, and listening activities — lives in one place. You don't need to download anything, log in anywhere, or create an account. It works on your Chromebook, phone, or tablet. Let me show you around."

## SLIDE 3 — The Hub Page
**Slide text:**
> The Hub = Your Home Base
> • Sections = groups of activities
> • Cards = individual activities (locked until that day)
> • Each card shows the day, type, and time

**Visual suggestion:** Screenshot of the hub with callouts on a section header, a locked card, and an unlocked card

**Speaker notes (1 min):**
"When you open the site, you land on the Hub page. Think of it like your class dashboard for this unit. Everything is organized into sections — Daily Warm-Ups, Grammar, Reading, Culture, Listening, and more. Each card is one activity. Some cards are locked until we reach that day — those show a lock icon. When a card is available, click it to open the activity. You'll see what type it is — Practice, Reading, Drill, Listening — and about how many minutes it takes."

---

## SLIDE 4 — Practice Activities
**Slide text:**
> Practice Activities ✏️
> • Multiple choice + fill-in questions
> • Click Submit → instant feedback
> • Your score appears on the right
> • Wrong answers show the correct answer

**Visual suggestion:** Screenshot of a practice activity with the score panel visible

**Speaker notes (1.5 min):**
"Practice activities are your grammar checks. They look like this — you'll see questions on the left and a score panel on the right. Answer all the questions, then click 'Check Answers.' The panel on the right will show your score and tell you which questions you got right or wrong. Wrong answers will reveal the correct answer so you can learn from it. You can keep working until you get them right — there's no penalty for trying again."

---

## SLIDE 5 — Reading Activities
**Slide text:**
> Reading Activities 📖
> • Two versions: Language Learner (L2) and Heritage Speaker
> • 🔊 Listen button → AI voice reads the full passage
> • Hover underlined words → see English translation
> • Click any word → hear it pronounced

**Visual suggestion:** Screenshot of a reading page with the Listen button highlighted, and a tooltip showing a translation

**Speaker notes (1.5 min):**
"Reading activities come in two versions — one for language learners and one for heritage speakers. Choose the one that fits you. On every reading page, look for the Listen button at the top. Click it and an AI voice will read the entire passage aloud in Spanish — you can follow along, pause, rewind 10 seconds, or fast-forward. For any word you don't know: hover over it and you'll see the English translation pop up. If you want to hear how a word sounds, click it. After you read, there are comprehension questions at the bottom — same as a practice activity."

---

## SLIDE 6 — Flashcards
**Slide text:**
> Flashcards 🃏
> • Click any card to flip it
> • Modes: Flashcard → Quiz → Match
> • Filter by category
> • 🔊 Click the speaker to hear the word

**Visual suggestion:** Screenshot of the flashcard showing front face and the speaker icon

**Speaker notes (45 sec):**
"The Flashcard link is always visible at the top of any page. Click it to open the full vocabulary review. You can flip cards by clicking them, switch to Quiz mode for multiple choice, or try Match mode. Use the category buttons at the top to focus on one set of vocabulary at a time. And — new this unit — each card has a speaker icon. Click it to hear the word pronounced clearly in Spanish. This is great for practicing before a quiz."

---

## SLIDE 7 — Daily Warm-Ups
**Slide text:**
> Daily Warm-Ups 🌅
> • Available at the start of each class (Day 2–14)
> • Reviews yesterday's grammar or vocabulary
> • 5 minutes — finish before class starts

**Speaker notes (45 sec):**
"Every day except Day 1, there's a warm-up waiting for you. It's 5 minutes, it reviews what we covered the day before, and it's the first thing you should open when you sit down. These are not graded for correctness — they're for review and practice. Getting them done consistently will make a real difference when it comes time for assessments."

---

## SLIDE 8 — Las 3 P's (Cultural Exploration)
**Slide text:**
> Las 3 P's — Colombian Culture 🇨🇴
> Productos → What Colombia creates
> Prácticas → What Colombians do
> Perspectivas → Why they do it
> Research. Discuss. Connect.

**Speaker notes (45 sec):**
"One of my favorite sections is Las 3 P's — Products, Practices, and Perspectives. This is where we move beyond grammar and into real cultural understanding. You'll explore what Colombia creates — foods, music, art. What Colombians do — traditions, daily life, celebrations. And most importantly, why — the values and beliefs behind those things. Some of these activities involve research, discussion prompts, and reflection. There are no wrong answers here."

---

## SLIDE 9 — Listening Section
**Slide text:**
> Listening 🎧
> • Music + cultural videos embedded directly
> • Word-order activities — hear it, build it
> • Podcast episodes — coming soon!

**Speaker notes (30 sec):**
"In the Listening section, you'll find embedded videos and music tied to what we're studying — cumbia, vallenato, Colombian culture. There's also a new word-order activity: you hear a sentence in Spanish and then arrange the words in the correct order by clicking. It's a great way to train your ear while reinforcing grammar. We'll also be adding podcast episodes you can listen to directly on the site."

## SLIDE 10 — Tips & Expectations
**Slide text:**
> A few expectations:
> ✅ Open the site at the start of every class
> ✅ Start with the Day's warm-up
> ✅ Use the Listen button before you read
> ✅ Hover words — don't just skip them
> 🚫 Don't rush to submit — read carefully

**Speaker notes (30 sec):**
"Quick expectations before we dive in. Open the site every class period — it's how we'll do warm-ups, practice, and readings. Use the tools that are built in: the audio, the hover translations, the flashcards. They're there to support you. Don't just click through to hit submit — slow down, use the audio, and actually read. This is language learning — the more you engage, the better you'll do."
